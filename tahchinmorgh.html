TAHCHIN MORGH

Tahchin Morgh is a stunning Persian rice cake layered with saffron-infused rice, yogurt, egg, and tender chicken. It’s baked until the bottom forms a golden, crispy crust known as *tahdig*. With its unique shape and rich flavors, Tahchin is a show-stopping dish often served at festive gatherings.
45
4

What You’ll Need:

  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 2-3 chicken thighs or breasts
  • 1 large onion (chopped)
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 1 large egg
  • 3 tbsp saffron water (brewed saffron in hot water)
  • 3 tbsp vegetable oil or melted butter
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional: Barberries and slivered pistachios for garnish

Just Follow These Steps:

  1. Rinse and soak the rice for 30 minutes. Boil in salted water until just tender. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a pan, sauté onions in oil. Add chicken, turmeric, salt, and pepper. Cook until chicken is fully cooked, then shred.
  3. In a large bowl, combine yogurt, egg, saffron water, and a pinch of salt. Mix well.
  4. Add the cooked rice to the yogurt mixture and fold to combine evenly.
  5. Grease a non-stick baking dish or pot. Spread half of the rice mixture on the bottom.
  6. Add the shredded chicken in an even layer, then top with the remaining rice mixture.
  7. Press down gently. Cover with foil or a lid and bake at 180°C (350°F) for 1 hour, until golden and crispy on the bottom.
  8. Let cool slightly, then invert onto a plate. Garnish with sautéed barberries or nuts if desired.
