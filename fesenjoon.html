FESENJOON

Fesenjoon is a classic Persian dish made with tender chicken (or duck) cooked in a rich, velvety sauce made from pomegranate molasses and ground walnuts. It's a perfect balance of savory, sweet, and tangy flavors, and is typically served with saffron rice.
45
4

What You’ll Need:

  • 4 chicken thighs (or duck legs)
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cups ground walnuts
  • 1/2 cup pomegranate molasses
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp turmeric
  • 1/2 tsp cinnamon
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 1 tbsp sugar (optional, depending on how sweet you prefer the dish)
  • Fresh pomegranate seeds (optional for garnish)
  • Saffron rice for serving

Just Follow These Steps:

  1. In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and sauté until golden brown and soft, about 5-7 minutes.
  2. Season the chicken with salt, pepper, and turmeric. Add the chicken to the pot and brown on both sides, about 5 minutes per side.
  3. Add the ground walnuts and cinnamon to the pot. Stir to coat the chicken with the walnut mixture.
  4. Pour in the chicken broth and pomegranate molasses. Bring to a simmer, cover, and cook on low heat for about 45 minutes, or until the chicken is tender and the sauce thickens.
  5. Adjust the sweetness by adding sugar if desired, and taste for seasoning, adding more salt or pepper as needed.
  6. Serve the Fesenjoon over saffron rice, garnished with fresh pomegranate seeds for an extra pop of color and flavor.
