CHICKEN DOPIAZA

Chicken Dopiaza is a flavorful Indian curry named for its generous use of onions—added at two stages. It features tender chicken simmered in a spiced tomato-onion gravy, perfect with rice or naan.
45
4

What You’ll Need:

  • 500g chicken (boneless or bone-in)
  • 3 large onions (2 sliced, 1 chopped)
  • 2 tbsp oil or ghee
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tsp ginger, grated
  • 2 tomatoes, pureed
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1/2 tsp chili powder (optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander for garnish

Just Follow These Steps:

  1. Heat oil in a pan and fry the sliced onions until golden and crispy. Set aside.
  2. In the same pan, add cumin seeds, then chopped onions, garlic, and ginger. Sauté until soft.
  3. Add pureed tomatoes, turmeric, coriander powder, and chili powder. Cook until the oil separates.
  4. Add chicken pieces and stir to coat in the masala. Cook for 10–12 minutes.
  5. Add water as needed, cover, and simmer until chicken is cooked through.
  6. Stir in fried onions and garam masala. Cook 2–3 more minutes.
  7. Garnish with coriander and serve with naan or rice.
