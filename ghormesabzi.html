GHORMEH SABZI

Ghormeh Sabzi is a beloved Persian herb stew made with tender chunks of lamb or beef, kidney beans, and an aromatic mix of herbs, including parsley, cilantro, and fenugreek. The stew is flavored with dried lime and served over fluffy saffron rice. It’s a fragrant, savory dish that is perfect for family meals.
45
4

What You’ll Need:

  • 500g lamb or beef (cubed)
  • 1 large onion (chopped)
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 cup kidney beans (soaked overnight)
  • 1 1/2 cups parsley (chopped)
  • 1 cup cilantro (chopped)
  • 1/2 cup fenugreek leaves (chopped, or substitute with dried fenugreek)
  • 1 tsp turmeric
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • 1 tsp salt (or to taste)
  • 2 dried limes (or 1 tsp lime powder)
  • 3-4 cups water or beef broth
  • Saffron rice for serving

Just Follow These Steps:

  1. In a large pot, heat vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and sauté until golden and soft, about 5-7 minutes.
  2. Add the lamb or beef cubes to the pot and brown on all sides. Stir in turmeric, black pepper, and salt, and cook for another 3 minutes.
  3. Add 3-4 cups of water or beef broth to the pot. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low. Cover and simmer for 45 minutes to 1 hour, or until the meat is tender.
  4. Meanwhile, in a separate pan, heat 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil and sauté the chopped herbs (parsley, cilantro, and fenugreek) for about 10-15 minutes, until the herbs release their fragrance and darken slightly.
  5. Add the sautéed herbs to the pot along with the soaked kidney beans. Stir well to combine.
  6. Place the dried limes in the stew. If using whole limes, gently pierce them with a fork before adding them to release their flavor. If using lime powder, add it at this stage.
  7. Cover the pot and let it simmer for another 1-2 hours, stirring occasionally. Add more water or broth as needed to keep the stew from drying out. The stew should be thick and the flavors well combined.
  8. Once the stew is ready, remove the dried limes and adjust the seasoning if needed. Serve hot with saffron rice.
