SWEDISH BLOOD PUDDING

Swedish blood pudding (*Blodpudding*) is a traditional Nordic dish made from pig's blood, rye flour, and spices. It’s typically served in slices, fried until crispy on the outside and soft inside, accompanied by lingonberry jam and boiled potatoes. A staple in many Swedish households, it's both hearty and nutritious.
45
4

What You’ll Need:

  • 1 liter fresh pig’s blood
  • 250g rye flour
  • 1 medium onion (grated)
  • 100ml dark syrup (or molasses)
  • 1 tsp ground allspice
  • 1/2 tsp ground white pepper
  • 1 tsp salt
  • Butter or oil for frying
  • To serve: Lingonberry jam & boiled potatoes

Just Follow These Steps:

  1. Preheat your oven to 175°C (350°F). Grease a loaf pan or blood pudding mold.
  2. In a large bowl, mix the blood, rye flour, grated onion, syrup, salt, and spices until smooth and well combined.
  3. Pour the mixture into the prepared pan. Cover with foil and bake for about 1 hour or until the pudding is firm and a knife inserted comes out clean.
  4. Let it cool completely. Slice into 1–2 cm thick pieces for serving.
  5. Fry slices in butter or oil on medium heat until crispy on the outside.
  6. Serve hot with lingonberry jam and boiled or mashed potatoes.
