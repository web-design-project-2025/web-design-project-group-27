PICKLED HERRING

Inlagd sill, or pickled herring, is a staple in Swedish cuisine — tangy, slightly sweet, and full of aromatic spices. Typically enjoyed with crispbread, potatoes, or as part of a festive smorgasbord.
20 + 24h
4–6

What You’ll Need:

  • 300g salted herring fillets (rinsed and soaked if needed)
  • 1 small red onion, thinly sliced
  • 5 allspice berries
  • 3 whole cloves
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 5 black peppercorns
  • 150ml white vinegar (12%)
  • 200ml water
  • 100g sugar
  • Fresh dill (optional, for garnish)

Just Follow These Steps:

  1. If using salted herring, soak the fillets in cold water for 12–24 hours, changing the water a few times.
  2. In a saucepan, combine vinegar, water, sugar, and all the spices. Bring to a boil, then let it cool completely.
  3. Layer the sliced onion and herring pieces in a clean glass jar.
  4. Pour the cooled pickling liquid over the herring to cover it completely.
  5. Seal the jar and refrigerate for at least 24 hours before serving.
  6. Serve chilled with boiled potatoes, crispbread, or at a Swedish holiday table.
